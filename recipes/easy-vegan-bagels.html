Easy Vegan Bagels

Description

This vegan bagel recipe is super simple and tastes just as delicious as non-vegan bagels. They have a weirdly lumpy shape to them, but you'll forget all about that once you bite into one and realize how delicious they are.

The boiling step is also optional, but I've found that it adds a yummy crunch to the outer shell of the bagel.

Tips for storage: These bagels will generally keep for a 5-7 days in a cool dry container like a bread box. You can freeze them if you want to keep them longer.
For best results when reheating:

  1. Put bagel in microwave with a small shotglass of water
  2. Heat for 15 seconds
  3. Slice in half and put into toaster on bagel setting or into a toaster oven, open face up

Ingredients

  • 1.25 cups lukewarm water
  • 2 tsp instant yeast
  • 1 Tbsp sugar
  • 3.5 cups flour
  • 1.25 tsp salt
  • 2 Tbsp coconut oil
  • 2 tsp baking soda
  • Desired toppings (optional)

Steps

  1. Rest and shape:
    1. Mix flour, yeast, sugar, and salt.
    2. Add water and knead until a soft dough is formed. Add more flour or water as necessary.
    3. Let dough rest for 1 hour to allow yeast to rise.
    4. Divide dough into 8 pieces.
    5. Push a finger through the middle to shape each piece into a bagel shape; keep in mind, bagels will increase in size when they are boiled and baked.
  2. Boil:
    1. Add baking soda to a pot of water.
    2. Boil each bagel for 1 minute each side, placing each on baking sheet after boiling.
    3. If necessary, melt coconut oil until it is spreadable, then brush each bagel with coconut oil.
    4. Add any toppings.
  3. Bake:
    1. Bake at 400 degrees for 20-25 minutes until lightly browned.
