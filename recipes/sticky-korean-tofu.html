Sticky Korean Tofu

Description

This sweet, spicy, sticky, crunchy tofu inspired by Korean flavors is to die for. It's got a crazy depth of both flavors and textures that will make it an instant favorite. I recommend serving it with white rice and broccoli, but you do you, boo.

A note about spice: If you're like me and you have a partner who can't stand a ton of heat, this is not the recipe to try with them. The heat in this recipe comes from the gochujang, which means it's not a "front of the mouth kind of spice. It's the kind that sneaks up on you and makes you break into cold sweats if you eat too much of it. Probably a 3/5 in terms of spice, but you won't know you've gone too far until it's too late.

Ingredients

  • Tofu
    • One pack of firm or extra firm tofu
    • 1.5 cups cornstarch
  • Sauce
    • 0.66 cups of ketchup
    • 0.5 cups gochujang
    • 0.5 cups maple syrup
    • 0.25 cups rice vinegar
    • 4 garlic cloves, minced

Steps

  1. Prep the Tofu
    1. Press and drain the tofu for at least 30min
  2. Sauce
    1. Saute garlic until fragrant
    2. Stir in ketchup, gochujang, maple syrup, and rice vinegar
    3. Simmer and cook until syrupy
    4. Remove from heat and let cool / thicken
  3. Tofu
    1. After pressing the tofu, cut into bite-sized cubes
    2. Toss in cornstarch until each piece is thinly dusted with cornstarch
    3. Add 2 Tbsp of oil to a pan and fry on medium high heat until tofu is brown and crispy
    4. Pour in 1 cup of the sauce so it steams and boils in the bottom of the pan
    5. Stir tofu in steaming sauce until it is coated and sauce is reduced
    6. This should result in a sticky, delicious, fragrant mess
    7. Let cool so you don't get third-degree burns in your mouth (the sticky sauce is like napalm)
    8. Serve with rice, veggies, and / or additional toppings like sesame seeds or green onion
